# Research Digest — 2026-05-19

## TL;DR

- **Capital consolidation:** Braintrust raised $80M at $800M valuation (Feb 2026); Langfuse acquired by ClickHouse ($400M Series D, Jan 2026); Arena (formerly LMArena) raised $150M at $1.7B (Jan 2026). Eval infrastructure money is real.
- **Closest lane entrant yet:** EvalArchitect (March 2026) — a PM-facing tool that generates a structured eval PRD from a feature description in 60 seconds. One-shot doc generation, no guided discovery. Not a charter workflow, but demand signal is loud.
- **Narrative momentum:** "Evals are the new PRD" is hitting critical mass (Braintrust blog, Reforge course, Designative.info, PromptQL). None of these tools walk non-engineers through goals → users → stories → what-to-measure. North Star's charter-first workflow remains uncontested as a product.

---

## 🚨 Direct Lane (Goal/Story Grounding) — Watch Closely

### EvalArchitect
**Source:** [AI Agent that Creates Quality Eval PRDs in 60 Seconds](https://aiforproduct.substack.com/p/ai-agent-that-creates-quality-eval) (March 16, 2026)

A PM-built tool that takes a feature description (one or two paragraphs) and generates a complete, structured eval PRD in under 60 seconds using Claude Sonnet. Hosted on GitHub Pages as a single file (~1,200 lines of code). Has 38 built-in validation checks on the output.

**Against the goal/story grounding rubric:**
- Does it walk a non-engineer from business goals → users → stories → what-to-measure? **No.** It's a one-shot prompt-to-document generator. The user provides a paragraph; the LLM extrapolates. No guided discovery, no structured conversation, no extraction of goals/users/stories, no charter artifact.
- Non-technical UX? **Partial.** Web UI a PM can use, but output quality depends entirely on the quality of the input paragraph.

**Read:** EvalArchitect proves demand for PM-accessible eval-spec tooling but stays at the document-generation layer. It skips the discovery phase entirely — precisely where North Star lives. A PM who has already thought through their product can get a quick eval spec doc; a PM who hasn't done that thinking gets a hallucinated one. North Star fills the upstream gap.

**Relevance to North Star:** Adjacent lane / early demand signal. Not a direct threat yet but worth watching for iteration.

## Competitive Threats — Eval Platforms

### Braintrust — $80M Series B, $800M Valuation
**Source:** [Braintrust articles](https://www.braintrust.dev/articles) / multiple roundup sources (Feb–March 2026)

Raised $80M led by Iconiq, with a16z, Greylock, Elad Gil. Launched a new Starter plan (free tier + usage-based) in March 2026. Published "Evals are the new PRD" blog and a dedicated "Evals for PMs" guide, positioning evals as specification artifacts, not just test runners. CEO Ankur Goyal did a podcast titled "PRDs Don't Work for AI Products. The CEO of Braintrust Explains What Does." (April 2026).

Braintrust's framework: **Data + Task + Scores** — a dataset, an evaluation function, and scorers. Strong eval-runner story, deep LangChain/SDK integration, prompt versioning, LLM-as-judge support.

**Goal/story grounding:** No. The PRD-replacement framing is about *running* evals serving as the spec, not about discovering what to evaluate from business goals and user stories.
**Non-technical UX:** Partial. Web UI exists, but configuration is engineering-heavy.

**Relevance:** Competitive threat (eval runner / observability layer) + methodology validation (evals-first narrative).

### Langfuse — Acquired by ClickHouse
**Source:** [ClickHouse acquires Langfuse blog](https://clickhouse.com/blog/clickhouse-acquires-langfuse-open-source-llm-observability) (Jan 16, 2026)

Architecture already ran on ClickHouse; acquisition integrates it natively. Roadmap and open-source commitment unchanged.

**Relevance:** Competitive threat (observability/eval platform) + consolidation signal. Langfuse moving into a $15B-valuation data infrastructure company shifts it toward enterprise durability. Makes it a harder long-term competitor for any platform that sells alongside it.

### Arena (formerly LMArena) — $150M Series A, $1.7B Valuation
**Source:** [LMArena raises $150M](https://siliconangle.com/2026/01/06/ai-evaluation-startup-lmarena-raises-150m-1-7b-valuation/) (Jan 6, 2026); [Rebrand to Arena](https://arena.ai/blog/series-a/) (Jan 28, 2026)

Crowdsourced LLM benchmarking platform (users compare two anonymous models side-by-side). 5M+ monthly active users, 60M+ conversations/month, $30M annualized consumption run rate. Funding led by Felicis + UC Investments, with a16z, Kleiner Perkins, Lightspeed.

**Goal/story grounding:** No. This is model benchmarking via human preference voting — entirely different from product-level eval definition.
**Non-technical UX:** Yes (it's a public web app). But the product is model comparison, not application eval.

**Relevance:** Adjacent lane (benchmarking/preference eval). Not a direct competitor to North Star but shows scale of capital entering the "AI quality measurement" category.

### DeepEval v4.0.0 (Confident AI) — May 8, 2026
**Source:** [DeepEval GitHub releases](https://github.com/confident-ai/deepeval/releases); [Confident AI platform](https://www.confident-ai.com/)

Major version 4 released May 8, 2026. Key new features: component-level granularity (score any step in an LLM pipeline — tools, retrievers, memories), multi-turn dataset support, agent-specific metrics (did the agent actually complete the task?), ArenaGEval (first pairwise comparison metric). Dashboard access for non-technical team members touted explicitly.

**Goal/story grounding:** No. Rich eval framework but users must bring their own criteria.
**Non-technical UX:** Partial. Dashboard readable by PMs; writing metrics or configuring evals still requires code.

**Relevance:** Competitive threat (eval framework + observability). The non-technical dashboard push is a positioning shift toward North Star's audience.

## Methodology Validation

### Reforge AI Evals Course
**Source:** [AI Evals Aren't Optional Anymore — Reforge](https://www.reforge.com/blog/ai-evals-course)

Reforge (the PM education platform) launched an "AI Evals" course explicitly for PMs and Senior PMs. Content: AI PRDs, golden datasets, eval rubrics, trace analysis, automated evaluators. Frames evals as "fundamentally an exercise in product thinking." Signals: (1) PM audience is actively seeking eval skills; (2) the market for non-technical eval tooling is expanding fast; (3) the gap between education and tooling is still wide.

**Relevance:** Methodology validation + indirect demand signal for North Star.

---

### Designative.info — "AI Evals for Designers and Product Managers: Why Criteria Matter Most"
**Source:** [designative.info, May 5, 2026](https://www.designative.info/2026/05/05/ai-evals-for-designers-and-product-managers-why-criteria-matter-most/)

A practitioner article making the case that eval criteria are fundamentally a product and design input, not an engineering one. Key quote: *"If you are a designer or PM on an AI product, ask to see your team's eval criteria — if you cannot read them, they were probably written without you."*

Argues that most evals are built around surface prompts rather than underlying user jobs, so systems appear to perform well while failing users in practice.

**Relevance:** Methodology validation. This article articulates North Star's core problem statement almost verbatim.

---

### Hamel Husain + Shreya Shankar — Updated Guidance on Eval-Driven Development
**Source:** [Hamel's Blog — LLM Evals FAQ](https://hamel.dev/blog/posts/evals-faq/) (Jan 2026); [Lenny's Newsletter podcast](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/why-ai-evals-are-the-hottest-new-skill)

New nuance in their January 2026 FAQ: they now say eval-driven development ("write evaluators before implementing features") "creates more problems than it solves" for LLMs because failure modes are infinite. Their recommendation: start with error analysis on real usage, write evaluators for errors you discover rather than errors you imagine.

**Implication for North Star:** This is not a repudiation of charter-first — it's a critique of specifying *micro-level metrics* in a vacuum. North Star addresses the *macro-level discovery* problem: what goals and stories should ground the eval at all. A charter derived from real user stories is closer to Hamel's "errors you discover" than to "imagined metrics." Consider making this distinction explicit.

**Relevance:** Methodology validation (the charter-first approach is upstream of the problem Hamel is critiquing) + positioning nuance to address.

**Source:** [Evals are the new PRD — Braintrust blog](https://www.braintrust.dev/blog/evals-are-the-new-prd); [PromptQL — Evals 101 for executives](https://promptql.io/blog/evals-101-for-executives-your-all-in-one-ai-prd-roadmap-and-roi-model)

## Research Papers

### Agent-RRM: Reasoning Reward Model for Agents
**Source:** [arXiv 2601.22154](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22154) (Jan 2026)

Introduces structured feedback for agentic trajectories: explicit reasoning trace + critique + overall score. Represents a shift toward *process-level* evaluation of agents, not just output quality.

**Relevance:** Methodology (process reward models could inform how North Star structures charter criteria for agent-based features). No immediate product impact.

---

### AgentPRM — Step-Wise Evaluation for LLM Agents
**Source:** [ACM Web Conference 2026](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3774904.3792551)

Lightweight actor-critic framework using Monte Carlo rollouts for step-by-step agent evaluation. Continuous improvement loop from agent interactions.

**Relevance:** Methodology validation for agent evaluation (increasingly relevant as North Star's users build agentic features).

---

### LLM-as-Judge Scoring Bias
**Source:** [arXiv 2506.22316v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.22316v1); [arXiv 2511.21140](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.21140)

Documented biases: position bias (~40% GPT-4 inconsistency), verbosity bias (~15% inflation), self-enhancement bias (5–7% boost). Calibration methods proposed: multiple evidence calibration, balanced position calibration, Rogan–Gladen estimator. The "How to Correctly Report LLM-as-a-Judge Evaluations" paper provides reporting standards.

**Relevance:** Methodology (North Star's CBAR rubric uses LLM-as-judge; these findings suggest the "Alignment" and "Rot" dimensions should flag when judge divergence rates exceed 20–25%).

## Positioning Hints

- **"The step before evals"** — The "evals are the new PRD" wave creates an obvious upstream hook: before you can write evals, you need to know what you're evaluating. North Star is where that clarity comes from. Lead with: *"Before your team writes a single eval, North Star helps you agree on what good looks like."*

- **Cite the Designative.info framing** — *"If you can't read your eval criteria, they were probably written without you."* This is the problem North Star solves, almost word for word. Consider using a version of this as a homepage hook.

- **Charter-first answers Hamel's critique** — Hamel warns against specifying imagined metrics. A CBAR charter derived from real user stories isn't imagined — it's grounded in actual product intent. Reframe CBAR as *"derived from user stories, not invented in a vacuum."*

- **Complementary, not competitive** — Langfuse and Braintrust compete at the instrumentation/scoring layer. North Star operates one level up. Positioning: *"We define what to measure. They measure it."* Makes North Star a handoff partner, not a threat to tools teams already use.

- **EvalArchitect demand signal** — A single-page GitHub Pages app getting Substack coverage shows real hunger for PM-accessible eval-spec tooling. Emphasize the guided discovery differentiator: one-shot generation gives you a document; North Star's conversation gives you confidence in the document.

---

## Proposed New Matrix Columns

- **Multi-turn simulation** — Does the tool support simulating multi-turn conversational scenarios for pre-deployment testing? (Distinct from single-turn eval runs; only Latitude and Maxim support this today.)
- **Export to eval runners** — Can the tool export its test cases / criteria directly to common eval frameworks (Braintrust, LangSmith, DeepEval)? This is where the "definition → execution" handoff matters.
- **Agent-specific metrics** — Does the tool score whether an agent completed its task (trajectory-level), not just whether a single output looked correct?
