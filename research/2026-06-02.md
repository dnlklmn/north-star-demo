## Research (New) — Measurement Imbalance in Agentic AI Evaluation (arXiv:2506.02064)

**Source:** [arXiv:2506.02064 — The Measurement Imbalance in Agentic AI Evaluation Undermines Industry Productivity Claims](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.02064) (June 2, 2026)

A systematic review of 84 papers (2023–2025) finds a structural bias at the core of how the industry evaluates agentic AI:

- **Technical metrics dominate (83%)** — task success rates, benchmark scores, latency, cost.
- **Human-centered assessment is peripheral (30%)** — user satisfaction, goal alignment, outcome quality for actual stakeholders.
- **Economic assessment is peripheral (30%)** — realized productivity, ROI, deployment value.
- **Only 15% of papers incorporate both technical and human dimensions simultaneously.**

The result: industry reports claiming agentic AI delivers double-digit productivity gains are built on evaluations that never measured what matters to the humans using the systems. Realized returns are "often less than 25% of forecasts." The paper frames this as "measurement imbalance" — a systemic bias that privileges the easily quantifiable over the actually relevant.

**Why this matters for North Star:** The paper's diagnosis is North Star's founding premise expressed in empirical terms. The charter conversation exists precisely to inject the human-centered dimensions (goals, users, stories, what good looks like for real stakeholders) *before* any technical metric is designed. Every agentic AI eval that starts with "task success rate" without a charter is a data point in the 83%. North Star's CBAR rubric operationalizes what the 15% does: it insists on measuring against human-defined standards, not just technical correctness.

**Against the goal/story grounding rubric:** Research paper only — no product, no PM-facing UX.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation (direct, empirically grounded).** Use in positioning: *"Industry evals measure whether your AI completed the task. North Star's charter defines whether it completed the **right** task, in the right way, for the right reasons — the human-centered dimensions that a new paper shows are missing from 70% of agentic AI evaluations."*

## Late Signal — AdaRubric: Task-Adaptive Rubrics for LLM Agent Evaluation (arXiv:2603.21362)

**Source:** [arXiv:2603.21362 — AdaRubric: Task-Adaptive Rubrics for LLM Agent Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21362) (March 22, 2026; not previously surfaced in this digest series)

[AdaRubric](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21362) from March 2026 (late signal, not previously covered): an LLM agent evaluator that generates task-specific rubric dimensions on the fly from task descriptions, then scores agent trajectories step-by-step with confidence-weighted per-dimension feedback.

Core finding: a fixed rubric cannot capture what matters for this task — code debugging demands *Correctness* and *Error Handling*; web navigation demands *Goal Alignment* and *Action Efficiency*. AdaRubric's task-adaptive dimensions achieve Pearson r=0.79 with human correlation (+0.16 over the best static baseline), with Krippendorff's α=0.83 (deployment-grade inter-rater reliability). DPO agents trained on AdaRubric preference pairs gain +6.8 to +8.5 pp task success vs. [Prometheus](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.01535) across three benchmarks.

**Charter-first parallel:** AdaRubric generates rubric dimensions from task descriptions; North Star generates rubric dimensions (CBAR axes) from user stories and business goals. The mechanism is the same — rubric dimensions should follow from the intent being evaluated, not be prescribed in advance — but North Star operates at the product definition layer, not the benchmark execution layer.

**This is the fourth institutional convergence on per-dimension rubric evaluation** (after [Los Alamos arXiv:2605.08061](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08061), [Microsoft Universal Verifier](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/), and [RubricEM/RubricRAG cluster](https://arxiv.org/html/2603.20882v1)), each from independent research groups.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation (rubric dimensionality, strong).** No Notion matrix entry needed — research paper only.
