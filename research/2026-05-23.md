## TL;DR

- [Red Hat AI 3.4](https://www.redhat.com/en/blog/inference-agentic-ai-scaling-enterprise-foundation-red-hat-ai-34) (announced May 11–13, 2026 at Red Hat Summit) ships an **Evaluation Hub** — a framework-agnostic, Kubernetes-native eval control plane for LLMs and agents — introducing the term "evaluation-driven development (EDD)" into the enterprise vocabulary; integration target, not a charter-first competitor.
- [RubricBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01562) (arXiv 2603.01562, March 4, 2026) provides the first empirical proof that LLMs cannot reliably generate their own evaluation rubrics — "a substantial capability gap between human-annotated and model-generated rubrics" — directly validating North Star's guided human-discovery approach.
- [ARC-AGI-3](https://arcprize.org/blog/arc-agi-3-launch) (March 25, 2026) launches an interactive benchmark where agents must discover winning conditions from scratch with no stated goals — a striking illustration at the frontier of why "knowing what to measure" is the hardest problem in AI evaluation.

## New Platform Release — Red Hat AI 3.4 Evaluation Hub

**Sources:** [Red Hat blog — From inference to agents: Scaling AI in the enterprise with Red Hat AI 3.4](https://www.redhat.com/en/blog/inference-agentic-ai-scaling-enterprise-foundation-red-hat-ai-34) (May 11, 2026); [Red Hat press release](https://www.redhat.com/en/about/press-releases/red-hat-unites-builders-and-operators-agentic-future-major-advancements-red-hat-ai) (May 13, 2026); [SiliconANGLE — Red Hat targets enterprise deployment with new version of its AI platform](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/11/red-hat-targets-enterprise-deployment-new-version-ai-platform/) (May 11, 2026)

[Red Hat AI 3.4](https://www.redhat.com/en/blog/inference-agentic-ai-scaling-enterprise-foundation-red-hat-ai-34), announced at Red Hat Summit 2026, introduces **Evaluation Hub**: a framework-agnostic unified AI evaluation control plane for LLMs, AI applications, and agents.

**What it is:**
- Replaces fragmented testing with a unified REST API + Kubernetes controller
- Curated and custom evaluation collections; dashboard with embedded MLflow; CLI + SDK access
- Uses Open Container Initiative (OCI) model cards for governance and an MCP server for agent-discoverable evaluations
- Integrates [Garak](https://github.com/leondz/garak) adversarial scanning (jailbreaks, prompt injection, bias) natively in the CI/CD pipeline
- Designed to scale from laptop to production pipeline via Kubernetes-native deployment

**Red Hat's framing:** They explicitly call this "evaluation-driven development (EDD)" — *"replacing subjective testing with concrete data and benchmarks to verify that models and agents are fully ready for production."* The EDD terminology mirrors TDD (test-driven development) and is entering the enterprise engineering vocabulary alongside [Vikas Goyal's "pre-build evals"](https://vikasgoyal.github.io/agentic/prebuildevals.html) framing (covered 2026-05-22).

**Against the goal/story grounding rubric:**
- Guided discovery from goals → users → stories → criteria? **No.** Evaluation Hub is a batch-evaluation execution layer. You must already know what to measure.
- Non-technical UX? **Partial.** MLflow dashboard is readable; the underlying tooling is Kubernetes-native and CLI/SDK-first. Enterprise IT persona, not PM persona.

**Pre-prod / prod path:**
- Pre-prod evals: **Yes** (dataset → curated eval run → score → benchmark)
- BYO dataset: **Yes** (custom evaluation collections)
- Generates synthetic data: **Unknown** (not described)
- Prod tracing: **Unknown** (focused on pre-prod benchmarking in available descriptions)
- LLM-as-judge: **Yes** (Garak-based AI scoring + custom scorers)
- Built-in scorers: **Yes** (curated evaluation collection library)
- Custom scorers: **Yes** (custom collection support)
- Prompt versioning: **Unknown**
- Goal/story grounding: **No**
- Non-technical UX: **Partial**

**Relevance to North Star: Integration target (enterprise/on-prem stack).** Evaluation Hub runs measurement; it does not define what to measure. For enterprise customers on the Red Hat/OpenShift stack who want to carry a North Star charter through to CI/CD, Evaluation Hub is the natural downstream runner — the same integration story as [Langfuse Experiments CI/CD](https://langfuse.com/docs/evaluation/experiments/experiments-ci-cd) (covered 2026-05-21) but targeting on-prem/sovereign-infrastructure buyers. The OCI model card governance layer and MCP server interface are specifically interesting: a North Star charter could be packaged as an OCI model card metadata artifact and surfaced as an MCP-discoverable eval spec. This is a concrete enterprise integration hypothesis worth tracking.

## Research Papers — Rubric Generation Is a Human Problem

### RubricBench: Human Rubrics vs. Model-Generated Rubrics

**Source:** [arXiv 2603.01562 — RubricBench: Aligning Model-Generated Rubrics with Human Standards](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01562) (March 4, 2026)

[RubricBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01562) is a curated benchmark of 1,147 pairwise comparisons specifically designed to measure whether model-generated evaluation rubrics align with human-annotated ones. It spans five domains: Chat, Instruction Following, STEM, Coding, and Safety. Construction uses a multi-dimensional filtration pipeline targeting hard samples with nuanced input complexity and misleading surface bias, annotated with expert-authored, atomic rubric criteria.

**Key findings:**

1. **Substantial capability gap:** Even state-of-the-art LLMs struggle to autonomously specify valid evaluation criteria. Model-generated rubrics lag considerably behind human-guided performance on discriminative, hard samples. The gap is not narrowing in proportion to general model capability improvement — rubric generation requires a form of criteria specification that models do not acquire implicitly.

2. **Surface-level bias as the failure mode:** Models tend to generate rubrics that capture surface signals (length, formatting, tone) rather than underlying behavioral correctness. Human experts reliably generate rubrics that decompose "good output" into atomic, independently verifiable constraints — a skill not yet replicable by LLMs at comparable quality.

3. **Discriminative complexity requirement:** The benchmark specifically targets cases where LLM rubrics look plausibly correct but fail to separate high- from low-quality outputs on hard cases. Easy cases mask the gap; the benchmark is designed to surface it.

**Relevance to North Star: Strongest methodology validation to date.** The paper directly empirically challenges the implicit assumption behind one-shot eval-spec generators: that an LLM can be prompted to produce trustworthy criteria without structured human elicitation. North Star's charter workflow uses guided conversation to extract goals, users, and stories from humans — treating the LLM as an extractor and structurer, not as a criteria originator. RubricBench validates this architectural choice. **Quote-ready:** *"Peer-reviewed research shows that even the best AI models fail to generate reliable evaluation rubrics without human guidance — which is exactly why North Star uses a guided discovery conversation to extract criteria from you, rather than inventing them."*

### RubricRAG: Rubric Generation Requires Domain Retrieval

**Source:** [arXiv 2603.20882 — RubricRAG: Towards Interpretable and Reliable LLM Evaluation via Domain Knowledge Retrieval for Rubric Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20882) (March 21, 2026)

[RubricRAG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20882) is a complementary paper to RubricBench. It identifies that **manually designing high-quality, query-specific rubrics is "labor-intensive and cognitively demanding and not feasible for deployment"** — and proposes a retrieval-augmented approach where rubric generation is grounded in retrieved domain knowledge rather than pure model inference.

**Key contribution:** Introduces three rubric-quality metrics (Rubric-BLEU, Rubric-ROUGE, Rubric-LLM-judge) and shows that domain-knowledge retrieval produces more interpretable and more accurate rubrics than zero-shot LLM generation.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation (complementary angle).** RubricRAG approaches the same problem as North Star from the automated side — trying to reduce the human burden of criteria specification — while North Star approaches it from the human side, reducing the barrier to high-quality elicitation. The paper's framing confirms the problem: rubric generation is hard even for experts, and domain context is required to get it right. North Star's structured conversation is the domain-context elicitation mechanism that RubricRAG is trying to approximate via retrieval. The two approaches could converge: North Star's charter could serve as the "retrieved domain knowledge" that a downstream RubricRAG-style system uses to generate granular per-query eval criteria.

## Methodology — "EDD" Enters the Enterprise Lexicon

**Source:** [Red Hat Developer — Eval-driven development: Build and evaluate reliable AI agents](https://developers.redhat.com/articles/2026/03/23/eval-driven-development-build-evaluate-ai-agents) (March 23, 2026)

Red Hat published an 8-stage evaluation framework for building reliable AI agents, explicitly naming the practice "eval-driven development (EDD)" — a deliberate analogy to test-driven development. The article argues that existing test frameworks don't work for agentic systems due to inherent nondeterminism, and that EDD forces teams to define success criteria before implementation begins.

Key framing: *"Many correct responses exist for any conversation turn, making comprehensive evaluations essential to ensure projects meet business goals."*

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation + terminology alignment.** "EDD" is the same conceptual framing as [Vikas Goyal's "pre-build evals"](https://vikasgoyal.github.io/agentic/prebuildevals.html) (covered 2026-05-22) but coming from an enterprise infrastructure vendor — Red Hat, not an indie blogger. When Red Hat brands a practice, enterprise engineering orgs follow. The term is now in the enterprise adoption funnel. North Star occupies the upstream layer of EDD: before you can do evaluation-driven development, you need to know what evaluation criteria should drive it. Align on "EDD" in enterprise-facing messaging.

## Benchmark Methodology — ARC-AGI-3

**Sources:** [ARC Prize — Announcing ARC-AGI-3](https://arcprize.org/blog/arc-agi-3-launch) (March 25, 2026); [arXiv 2603.24621 — ARC-AGI-3: A New Challenge for Frontier Agentic Intelligence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24621)

[ARC-AGI-3](https://arcprize.org/blog/arc-agi-3-launch) introduces a fundamentally new benchmark paradigm: hundreds of interactive, turn-based environments where AI agents must discover the rules, objectives, and winning conditions on their own — **no instructions, no stated goals**. Human baseline: 100%. Frontier AI baseline: 0.51%.

The benchmark was co-announced by François Chollet (ARC-AGI creator) and Sam Altman (OpenAI CEO) at Y Combinator on March 25, 2026. ARC Prize 2026 is live with $2M+ in prize money.

**What ARC-AGI-3 reveals:** The frontier of agentic intelligence is not "can the AI complete tasks?" but "can the AI figure out what the task is?" The 99.5% performance gap between humans and frontier models on ARC-AGI-3 is almost entirely attributable to goal discovery — not execution capability.

**Against the goal/story grounding rubric:**
- ARC-AGI-3 is a benchmark/research initiative, not a product. No Notion entry needed.
- It does not compete with North Star's charter workflow.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation (frontier context).** ARC-AGI-3's design philosophy mirrors North Star's core problem statement at the frontier level: the hardest unsolved problem in AI evaluation is defining what "winning" looks like — not running the evaluation once you know. North Star makes this problem tractable for product teams by guiding humans through structured discovery. Use as a concrete anchor when explaining the charter-first thesis: *"Frontier researchers at ARC Prize find the same bottleneck — the hardest part is telling the AI what it should be optimizing for. North Star is that conversation, for product teams."*
