[Truesight](https://truesight.goodeyelabs.com/) was added to the Notion competitor matrix on May 12 (pre-digest) but has not appeared in prior digests. Key facts:

- **Goal/Story Grounding: No** — starts at "quality bar" definition (what to measure), not at goals/stories/intent; their stated position: *"Build eval criteria before looking at your data? You will measure the wrong things."*
- MCP integration for Claude, Cursor, Windsurf, VS Code
- Pricing: Solo / Teams / Enterprise tiers (launched out of closed beta)

**Why Hamel's endorsement matters:** Hamel is the single most cited practitioner voice for EDD methodology. His backing of Truesight means the EDD community's default recommendation for a non-technical eval tool points to data-first criteria discovery. This is the sharpest external challenge yet to North Star's methodological premise — if Hamel tells PMs to "start with examples, not intent," the pre-eval discovery layer that North Star occupies faces a direct counter-narrative from the space's most credible voice.

**The fault-line in one sentence:** Truesight says "Show me examples of good and bad output and I'll build your criteria." North Star says "Tell me your goals and user stories and I'll tell you what criteria you need — before you have enough examples to learn from."

**Relevance to North Star: Competitive threat (methodology fault-line made explicit by the field's leading practitioner voice).**

## METHODOLOGY VALIDATION — LLM Safety Judges Exhibit Rigid Internal Priors

**Source:** [arXiv:2606.07874 — "LLM Judges Exhibit Rigid Priors Limiting Contextual Safety"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07874) (Anissa Alloula et al., submitted June 5, 2026)

[arXiv:2606.07874](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07874) evaluates LLMs as automated safety judges along two dimensions: (1) ability to absorb new in-context information, and (2) steerability when deployment context or safety definitions contradict their internal training. Finding: judges can learn from demonstrations but resist adjusting evaluations when context contradicts internal priors. A judge that appears to generalize from examples silently reverts to its training distribution when faced with changing safety specifications.

The implication for evaluation architecture: automated safety judges produce stable scores, but stability and correctness are not the same thing. When a deployment has context-specific safety definitions, rigid-prior judges score confidently against the wrong criteria. This is a calibration problem that cannot be solved by prompt engineering alone — it requires explicit quality definitions provided before the judge runs, which is exactly what a charter supplies.

This is the tenth entry in the judge-reliability cluster following [EDIT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.06350), [LongJudgeBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01629), [SenseJudge](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.03189), [arXiv:2606.09376](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09376), and six prior papers.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation.** Rigid priors add a new failure mode to the judge-reliability argument — judges don't just exhibit style/verbosity bias, they also fail to update on deployment-specific quality definitions. The charter is the mechanism for injecting those definitions before the judge runs.

## METHODOLOGY VALIDATION — SOTA Judges Fail on Real Human Reasoning (RealMath-Eval)

**Source:** [arXiv:2606.10254 — "RealMath-Eval: Why SOTA Judges Struggle with Real Human Reasoning"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10254) (submitted June 8, 2026)

[arXiv:2606.10254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10254) constructs a benchmark of 224 real high-school exam responses (human-generated, not synthetic) and tests whether SOTA LLM judges can grade them against expert human rubrics. Mean squared error against expert graders is ~2.96 — substantially higher than near-zero error the literature assumes for model-generated benchmark datasets. The gap between automated-eval performance on synthetic benchmarks and performance on real human output is large and systematic.

The diagnostic: LLM judges are calibrated against model-generated text. When the evaluation target is real human output in a domain with an expert-defined quality bar, SOTA judges fail because their calibration does not transfer. Domain-specific quality bars require explicit expert grounding — not just deploying a SOTA judge.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation.** Empirical evidence that "good enough for benchmark" is not "good enough for your users." Domain-expert quality bars require the kind of explicit criteria definition that a charter captures before any judge is deployed.

## Positioning Hints

- **The Hamel fault-line is now citation-ready.** [Truesight's position](https://truesight.goodeyelabs.com/) — "Build eval criteria before looking at your data? You will measure the wrong things" — is the clearest articulation of the competing worldview. North Star's counter: criteria built from examples only measure whether output resembles past examples; criteria built from goals and user stories measure whether output serves the actual intent. The charter is the mechanism that makes the difference visible before you have any data to build from.

- **Adopt "contextual evals" — then extend it.** [Hamel Husain](https://x.com/HamelHusain/status/2013622027135996100) frames Truesight as doing "contextual evals (not generic metrics)." North Star can use and extend this language: a charter-first workflow produces *contextually grounded criteria* — criteria derived from the specific goals, users, and stories of the deployment, not just from deployment examples. The charter makes context *explicit and auditable*, not implicit and example-dependent.

- **Judge rigid priors + RealMath-Eval together close the argument.** [arXiv:2606.07874](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07874) (judges ignore context changes) and [arXiv:2606.10254](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10254) (judges fail on real human output) are now ten papers deep in the judge-reliability cluster. The through-line: automated judges cannot reliably approximate domain-specific quality bars unless those bars are explicitly supplied. The charter is that supply mechanism.
