**Sources:** [Galtea raises $3.2M — tech.eu (March 25, 2026)](https://tech.eu/2026/03/25/galtea-lands-32m-to-cut-costly-ai-testing-delays/) | [Galtea product overview](https://galtea.ai/solution/product-overview)

[Galtea](https://galtea.ai/) is a [Barcelona Supercomputing Center](https://www.bsc.es/) spin-off funded in March 2026 ($3.2M seed from [42CAP](https://42cap.com/) and [Mozilla Ventures](https://mozilla.vc/)). Core workflow: a user writes a behavioral description of how their AI agent is intended to behave → Galtea automatically generates adversarial test cases and synthetic user simulations against that specification.

**The two paths assessment:**
- *Pre-prod path:* **Yes** — behavioral description → adversarial test generation → eval runs before deployment.
- *Prod path:* **Unknown** — no evidence of live-traffic tracing or online scorers in available materials.

**Vs. North Star — three-bullet read:**
1. **Input abstraction:** Galtea requires a written behavioral description — the user must arrive with a spec. North Star *generates* the behavioral specification through guided conversation (goals → users → stories → charter). Galtea's input is North Star's output.
2. **Output structure:** Galtea produces adversarial test cases (pass/fail orientation). North Star produces a labeled dataset organized by a structured multi-dimensional rubric (CBAR), which supports systematic gap analysis beyond pass/fail.
3. **Non-technical UX:** Writing a behavioral description is more accessible than writing evals directly, but still requires structured written thought. North Star's charter is co-produced in conversation — users don't need to know what a behavioral specification is.

**Relevance to North Star: Adjacent lane (closest found in 14 days of research).** Monitor for: whether Galtea adds a guided discovery step upstream of the behavioral description. That would be the first direct entry into North Star's lane.

## Research (New) — ProjectionBench: Contamination-Resistant Eval via Progressive Disclosure (arXiv:2605.30284)

**Source:** [arXiv:2605.30284 — ProjectionBench: Evaluating Scientific Hypothesis Generation in LLMs Under Progressive Information Disclosure](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30284) (Lew, Cao, Buehler; Unreasonable Labs; May 28, 2026)

[ProjectionBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.30284) introduces progressive information disclosure as a contamination-resistant eval methodology: models receive only a research topic and question initially; technical details of the target study are progressively revealed across rounds. Hypotheses generated at each stage are scored against original paper conclusions via atomic-claim semantic similarity.

**The charter-first parallel:** Contamination resistance follows from the same principle as charter-first development — evaluation criteria defined *before* the model sees the full answer cannot be contaminated by answer-specific patterns. A charter written before dataset generation cannot be reverse-engineered from the dataset. The pre-commitment to evaluation criteria (the charter) is what makes the evals valid, not just the eval scores themselves.

**Against the goal/story grounding rubric:** Research paper (Unreasonable Labs); no product, no PM-facing UX.

**Relevance to North Star: Methodology validation (contamination resistance, direct).** Use in positioning: *"Defining your evaluation criteria before generating your dataset isn't just best practice — it's what makes your evals contamination-resistant. North Star's charter conversation happens before a single test case is written."*
